What Does it Do?

The Loyola Bitcoin Notary is a proof-of-concept project to demonstrate how a blockchain-based service can emulate a real-life notary. When you upload a document, a cryptographic hash of that document is created. The hash (a series of letters and numbers) is unique to that document. Changing even one letter in the document would change the hash. We take the hash of the document and use Keybase to digitally sign the hash with your personal signature. We take that signed text, create a new hash from it, and then upload it to Proof of Existence. Once you pay the fee (roughly $2 USD), the hash is saved to the bitcoin blockchain.

Who is Behind It?

The Loyola Bitcoin Notary is a project of the Technology and Legal Innovation Clinic at the Loyola College of Law, New Orleans. The project was created by 3L law students Devyn Glass, Stephanie Toyos, Kris Beralo, and Nina Turk, under the supervision of Clinic Professor Judson Mitchell.

The project borrows heavily from the ideas of Manuel Araoz and uses his Proof of Existence API to record data to the blockchain.

Disclaimer

This project is a proof-of-concept, meaning it is offered only to demonstrate what can be done with new technologies. You should not use it for actual legal documents, nor rely on it in any way for any type of real-world transaction. This software is offered for educational purposes only.
